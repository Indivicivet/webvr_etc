================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: ZEN BUBBLE POPPER (0005)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A relaxing, satisfying game where the player stands in a peaceful garden setting 
and pops shiny, translucent soap bubbles floating up around them. It is 
designed for pure casual satisfaction.

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: We initially tried a 100% Gaze-Only control scheme. However, this 
  was updated to support WebXR tracked controllers (e.g. Meta Touch, Vive, Index) 
  using a laser pointer for aiming/popping, while retaining gaze-only interaction 
  as an automatic fallback if controllers are not detected.
* Interaction: Popping is instant. When the laser pointer (or gaze cursor) 
  overlaps a bubble and the trigger is pulled (or hovered on gaze), it pops 
  instantly (0ms delay).
* Locomotion: We initially tried adding locomotion (grip-steer and thumbstick controls) 
  to allow the player to walk around the garden. However, it was removed as movement 
  is unnecessary for bubble popping and could lead to issues like analog stick drift 
  causing runaway camera drift. The camera is now kept completely stationary (with 
  WASD controls disabled on desktop) to ensure maximum comfort and prevent players 
  from wandering away from the spawn area.
* Field of View (FoV): We initially tried spawning bubbles in a narrow stream 
  directly in front of the player (Z between -3 and -5). To improve visual depth 
  and scale, we expanded bubble spawning so they scatter closer and further away 
  (X between -4.0 and 4.0, Z between -1.5 and -9.0), with a portion originating 
  from the central pond and others spawning from the surrounding grass.
* Comfort: Slow-moving objects, stationary camera, and a calm, static background 
  make it highly comfortable and motion-sickness free.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Background: A stylized, soft-colored low-poly skybox representing a sunny field.
* Environment: A peaceful meadow containing cherry blossom trees, pine trees, 
  and mountains. Features a central cobblestone pond decorated with lily pads 
  and lotus flowers, surrounded by low-poly boulders, glowing Japanese stone 
  lanterns (Toro), and colorful flower patches.
* Bubbles: Shiny spheres using a standard material with high reflectivity 
  (roughness: 0.05, metalness: 0.95) and transparency. 
  When popped, they emit a quick splash of tiny water droplet particles.
* HUD: A glassmorphic screen overlay (for 2D players) and a curved in-world 
  glass dashboard (for VR players) displaying current score, multiplier, and time.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Start Screen: A single giant bubble containing the word "POPMETOPLAY" floats in 
  front. Gaze at it or shoot/trigger it to begin. (Fades out the 2D panel).
2. Gameplay: Streams of bubbles rise from the pond and surrounding meadow.
3. Interaction: The player points and pulls the trigger (or uses camera gaze) 
   to pop bubbles:
   - Play a high-quality, organic "pop" sound effect.
   - Trigger a particle burst.
   - Remove the bubble entity and add points.
4. Special Bubbles:
   - Golden Bubble: Grants a temporary 2x score multiplier.
   - Star Bubble: Pops all other bubbles currently on screen.
5. Mode: 60-second time attack. Try to get the highest score possible.
6. Game Over: A 3D restart bubble floats in front. Pop it to play again without 
   exiting VR.

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Setup:
  `<a-scene>`
    `<a-entity id="rig" position="0 0 0">`
      `<a-camera id="camera" position="0 1.6 0" look-controls="pointerLockEnabled: false" wasd-controls="enabled: false">`
        `<a-cursor id="cursor" fuse="false" raycaster="objects: .bubble, .vr-btn"></a-cursor>`
      `</a-camera>`
      `<a-entity id="left-hand" laser-controls="hand: left" raycaster="objects: .bubble, .vr-btn; far: 50"></a-entity>`
      `<a-entity id="right-hand" laser-controls="hand: right" raycaster="objects: .bubble, .vr-btn; far: 50"></a-entity>`
    `</a-entity>`
  `</a-scene>`

* Spawning:
  Use a JavaScript generator to spawn bubble entities over the pond or scattered 
  throughout the garden.
  Animate position upward:
  `<a-sphere class="bubble" radius="0.15" material="shader: standard; roughness: 0.05; metalness: 0.95; opacity: 0.55; color: #81ecec; transparent: true"></a-sphere>`

* Event Listeners:
  Tracks clicks (instant trigger click or touch/gaze click) to pop:
  `bubbleEl.addEventListener('click', () => {`
    `bubble.pop();`
  `});`
