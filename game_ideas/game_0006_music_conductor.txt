================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: GAZE AUDIO CONDUCTOR (0006)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
An interactive musical toy/game. The player stands in the center of a neon stage. 
In front of them are 4 floating musical nodes (Drums, Bass, Synth, and Lead). 
By gazing at different nodes, the player unmutes or applies audio filters (like 
lowpass sweeps) to those tracks, allowing them to dynamically mix a looping song 
just by looking around.

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: We initially tried a 100% gaze-responsive setup with no buttons, but 
  it needed changing to allow active VR controller laser aiming and trigger clicks 
  (as well as mouse clicks on desktop) to enable tactile and expressive play.
* Field of View (FoV): We initially tried placing the nodes closer (around z=-2.7m), 
  but it needed changing to fit within smaller FoV headsets. Nodes are now 
  positioned further away (z=-3.5m to -3.9m) and scaled down by ~20%.
* Comfort: Completely stationary. Gentle audio-reactive animations on the nodes 
  correlate to the volume/frequency of the respective track.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Background: A dark, atmospheric concert stage look with neon spotlight beams.
* Audio Nodes: Four distinct, glowing abstract geometries representing the stems:
  - Drum Node: A pulsing octahedron (pulsates with the kick drum).
  - Bass Node: A low, heavy horizontal ring that ripples.
  - Synth Node: A glowing matrix of points.
  - Lead Node: A sharp, rotating diamond.
* Laser Pointer & Target Halo: A neon laser guide line extending from the player's 
  controllers. When aimed at an instrument node, the laser color snaps to that 
  node's signature color, and a spinning holographic ring (pointer halo) appears 
  around the target node to indicate focus.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Setup: A multi-track electronic sequence runs. Initially, all stems are muted 
   except the Drums (at 15% floor volume).
2. Focus: When the player gazes at a node or points a controller at it:
   - The node lights up and begins to spin faster.
   - The volume ramps up smoothly over ~3 seconds.
   - An audio lowpass filter sweeps open.
3. Looking Away: When the player looks away:
   - We initially tried a fast volume fade-down (e.g. 0.6s), but it needed changing 
     to allow more complex layered mixing. Tracks now fade out gradually over ~10s.
4. Trigger Enhancement: Pointing a controller at a node and clicking the trigger 
   briefly plays an enhanced, syncopated harmonizing overlay riff for 2 bars (32 steps), 
   accompanied by a neon white visual flash on the floor rings. Additionally, an 
   expanding holographic shockwave sphere bursts outwards from the node and a floating 
   "BOOSTED!" text label rises and fades out to provide clear feedback.
5. "Solo" Challenge Mode: A customer order or "recipe" sequence flashes on screen 
   (e.g., "DRUM + LEAD"). The player must coordinate their gaze/aim to maintain the 
   correct combination of active instruments to match the rhythm prompt.

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Web Audio API:
  We initially tried loading static mp3 loops, but it needed changing to ensure 
  perfect sample-accurate sync and flexible riff layering. We now synthesize all 
  stems (drums, bass, synth, lead) in real-time using a custom Web Audio API 
  synthesis oscillator engine.

* A-Frame Elements:
  `<a-scene>`
    `<a-camera>`
      `<a-cursor raycaster="objects: .instrument" fuse="false"></a-cursor>`
    `</a-camera>`
    
    `<a-entity class="instrument" id="drum-node" position="-1.5 1.5 -3" geometry="primitive: octahedron" material="color: red"></a-entity>`
    `<a-entity class="instrument" id="bass-node" position="-0.5 1.5 -3" geometry="primitive: torus" material="color: blue"></a-entity>`
    `<a-entity class="instrument" id="synth-node" position="0.5 1.5 -3" geometry="primitive: box" material="color: green"></a-entity>`
    `<a-entity class="instrument" id="lead-node" position="1.5 1.5 -3" geometry="primitive: tetrahedron" material="color: yellow"></a-entity>`
  `</a-scene>`

* Scripting Fade Logic:
  Use raycaster events to trigger volume changes:
  `let gainNode = audioContext.createGain();`
  
  `el.addEventListener('raycaster-intersected', () => {`
    `// Ramp volume up to 1.0 over 0.3 seconds`
    `gainNode.gain.linearRampToValueAtTime(1.0, audioContext.currentTime + 0.3);`
  `});`
  `el.addEventListener('raycaster-intersected-cleared', () => {`
    `// Ramp volume down to 0.1 over 0.6 seconds`
    `gainNode.gain.linearRampToValueAtTime(0.1, audioContext.currentTime + 0.6);`
  `});`
