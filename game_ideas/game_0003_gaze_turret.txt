================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: GAZE TURRET DEFENDER (0003)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
You are an orbital defense operator in a stationary space turret. Space debris 
and meteors are falling towards your station. You must target and melt them with 
your plasma cannon by pointing your VR controller (or looking directly at them in 
desktop mode).

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: we initially tried gaze lock-on auto-fire without controllers, but it 
  needed changing to support VR controllers (via the WebXR API) for a more immersive 
  and natural pointing experience. In desktop mode, it falls back to gaze/reticle 
  lock-on. When controllers are active, the center gaze reticle is hidden.
* Field of View (FoV): Crucially limited to a narrow front cone vertically (from 
  Y=0.8 to Y=2.6) and horizontally (X=-5.75 to X=5.75) at Z=-35, making it playable 
  on smaller FoV headsets without causing neck strain.
* Comfort: Completely stationary. Debris moves at a slow-to-moderate velocity, 
  and the distant starfield remains static, keeping the experience highly comfortable.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Cockpit Frame: A dark metal frame surrounds the edges of the camera view to ground 
  the player.
* Debris: Low-poly asteroids and glowing metal space junk (old satellites). They 
  turn bright glowing orange/red as they take laser damage.
* Laser Cannon Mounts: Left and right gun barrels visible in the lower corners 
  of the screen.
* Lasers: we initially tried thin neon green lines, but it needed changing to look 
  more premium. It now uses double-layered 3D cylinder beams (a green glowing sheath 
  with a hot white core) that stretch from the barrels to the target.
* Impact effects: Laser contact sparks spray colorful embers (orange/yellow for 
  asteroids, cyan/white for satellites) from the point of impact.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Start Screen: Look at the console screen or click it with your VR controller 
   to boot the system.
2. Threat Wave: Asteroids spawn at Z = -35 at random X/Y coordinates in front of 
   the player and glide forward.
3. Locking On: Player aligns their VR controller (or the camera reticle in desktop mode) 
   onto an asteroid.
4. Firing: The raycaster detects the hit:
   - Double-layered 3D volumetric cylinder lasers are drawn from the gun barrels to the asteroid.
   - Laser contact sparks spray from the target.
   - The asteroid begins to crack and glow.
   - If held for 0.8 seconds, the asteroid explodes into particles (gains points).
5. Shield Impact: Any asteroid that crosses Z = -1.5 hits the shield:
   - Screen flashes blue/red.
   - Shield health decays by 25%.
6. Game Over: Shield reaches 0%.

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Raycaster Camera & Controllers:
  `<a-entity id="rig">`
    `<a-camera id="camera">`
      `<a-cursor id="gaze-cursor" raycaster="objects: .target"></a-cursor>`
    `</a-camera>`
    `<a-entity id="left-hand" laser-controls="hand: left" raycaster="objects: .target"></a-entity>`
    `<a-entity id="right-hand" laser-controls="hand: right" raycaster="objects: .target"></a-entity>`
  `</a-entity>`

* Lasers (3D Volumetric Cylinders):
  We use double-layered cylinders and position, scale, and rotate them using 
  quaternions and vector midpoint math to connect start (barrel) and end (target) points:
  `let distance = startVec.distanceTo(endVec);`
  `let midpoint = new THREE.Vector3().addVectors(startVec, endVec).multiplyScalar(0.5);`
  `containerEl.object3D.position.copy(midpoint);`
  `containerEl.object3D.scale.set(1, distance, 1);`
  `let direction = new THREE.Vector3().subVectors(endVec, startVec).normalize();`
  `let quaternion = new THREE.Quaternion().setFromUnitVectors(new THREE.Vector3(0, 1, 0), direction);`
  `containerEl.object3D.quaternion.copy(quaternion);`

* Spark Particles:
  An A-Frame component `impact-spark` is registered to emit short-lived, colorful 
  drifting particles at the target intersection point.
