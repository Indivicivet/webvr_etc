================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: CHROMATIC GAZE MATCHER (0004)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A cozy, casual color-matching puzzle game. The player faces a floating cluster of 
colored cubes surrounding a central "target sphere". The target sphere pulses with 
a specific color (e.g., neon purple). The player must match the colored cubes to the 
target sphere to clear them from the field before the time runs out.

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: We initially tried 100% Gaze-Only (where a gaze fuse cursor acts as the 
  action trigger), but it was updated to support WebXR VR controllers (using laser 
  pointer rays and trigger clicks) as the primary input when available. It dynamically 
  falls back to the 400ms head-gaze fuse cursor if no controllers are connected, 
  ensuring accessibility across all headsets.
* Field of View (FoV): Optimized for smaller fields of view. We initially positioned 
  the orbital cubes at a 0.7m radius, but it was scaled down to a compact 0.5m radius 
  circle (with cube sizes reduced from 0.14m to 0.11m) so that the entire board fits 
  snuggly within a smaller FOV and reduces required eye strain or excessive head movement.
* Comfort: Completely stationary camera position. The orbit plane tilt and rotation 
  provide dynamic 3D depth, but the player remains at (0, 0, 0), avoiding any motion comfort concerns.
* Background Music: Continuously playing ambient zen chord swells synthesized via 
  the Web Audio API (sine waves with slow attack and release), keeping the audio 
  experience self-contained and cozy.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Background: A dark, soft gradient background with floaty dust particles to provide 
  depth and scale.
* Target Sphere: A large, glowing glass sphere in the center of the viewport that 
  slowly breathes light in the target color.
* Puzzle Grid: 8 smaller cubes orbiting the target sphere, colored randomly in 
  shades of cyan, purple, magenta, and yellow.
  - We initially tried simple 2D rotation of the orbital group around the Z-axis only. 
    However, to make gameplay more dynamic and immersive as players progress, we updated 
    it: in Wave 1, the orbit plane remains flat in the X-Y plane (no tilt). In Wave 2 and 
    beyond, the orbit plane tilts and rotates around all three axes (X, Y, Z), with the 
    rotation speed increasing dynamically with each round (wobbling in 3D), while individual 
    cubes continue to spin at their normal rate on their local axes.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Start Screen: Trigger the glowing "LOOK TO START / START" ring in VR or click "Begin Match" on desktop.
2. Wave Start: The target sphere turns a specific color (e.g., Cyan). The 8 orbital 
   cubes are assigned random colors (3 cyan, 5 others).
3. Player Action: Player aims a VR controller ray or looks at a Cyan cube.
4. Correct Match: If the player successfully clicks or fuses a matching cube:
   - Play a correct chime sound and trigger a controller haptic rumble.
   - Cube scales down to zero and is removed from the active count.
5. Incorrect Match: If the player selects a non-matching cube:
   - Play a soft buzzer sound and trigger a double controller haptic warning pulse.
   - The cube shakes and locks out for 1 second, and the score multiplier resets.
6. Wave Complete: Once all matching cubes are cleared, the remaining cubes spin rapidly, 
   shuffle, and a new target color is set.
7. Timer: The player has 20 seconds per wave. Clearing a matching cube adds 2 seconds.

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Scene Layout:
  `<a-scene>`
    `<a-entity id="rig" position="0 0 0">`
      `<a-camera id="camera" look-controls="pointerLockEnabled: false" ...>`
        `<a-cursor id="gaze-cursor" fuse="true" fuse-timeout="400" ...></a-cursor>`
      `</a-camera>`
      `<a-entity id="left-controller" laser-controls="hand: left" raycaster="objects: .target" ...></a-entity>`
      `<a-entity id="right-controller" laser-controls="hand: right" raycaster="objects: .target" ...></a-entity>`
    `</a-entity>`
    
    `<!-- Central Target -->`
    `<a-sphere id="target-sphere" position="0 1.4 -2" radius="0.25" ...></a-sphere>`
    
    `<!-- Orbital Cubes (arranged in a circle of radius 0.5 around target) -->`
    `<a-entity id="puzzle-group" position="0 1.4 -2">`
      `<a-box class="color-cube" position="0.5 0 0" width="0.11" ...></a-box>`
      `<a-box class="color-cube" position="0.354 0.354 0" width="0.11" ...></a-box>`
      `<!-- Repeat for other 6 coordinates -->`
    `</a-entity>`
  `</a-scene>`

* Scripting State:
  - Colors array: `['#00f3ff', '#ff007f', '#ffff00', '#9d00ff']` (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Purple).
  - Target Color: randomly selected from the array.
  - Plane Rotation Accumulator: `game.planeRotation = { x: 0, y: 0, z: 0 }`.
  - Ambient Music Synth: soft swells triggered via periodic `AudioContext` sine node creation.
  - Multi-Axis Rotation: frame-rate independent rotation in `tick`:
    `let rx = (wave - 1) * 3;`
    `let ry = (wave - 1) * 4;`
    `let rz = 10 + (wave - 1) * 5;`
    `game.planeRotation.x = (game.planeRotation.x + rx * dt) % 360;`
    `// Apply combined angles to puzzleGroup rotation attribute`
