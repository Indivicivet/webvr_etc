================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: GYRO TUNNEL DODGE (0002)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
An endless-runner style game where the player pilots a virtual glider down a 
glowing hexagonal wireframe tunnel. The player must steer to dodge oncoming red 
energy barriers by tilting their head left or right.

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: Steer via Gyro-based head tilt (yaw/roll). We initially tried a gaze-only 
  input model without any controller support for menu interactions, but it needed 
  changing to include WebXR VR controller support (laser pointer controls) for 
  clicking the Start/Restart buttons to make it fully playable in VR. Desktop fallback 
  supports mouse position tracking and keyboard (A/D or Arrows).
* Field of View (FoV): Optimized for low-FoV headsets (60-70 degrees). Since the 
  tunnel converges to a single point in the distance directly in front of the camera, 
  all upcoming obstacles are visible immediately.
* Comfort: Moving forward in VR can cause motion sickness. To combat this, a static 
  cockpit HUD is rendered on-screen to provide a stable visual anchor. The player 
  does not actually move forward; rather, the obstacles move toward the player.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Tunnel: A hexagonal tube composed of glowing cyan wireframe rings spaced 
  regularly. The rings slowly rotate to create a sense of speed.
* Obstacles: Solid red translucent barriers that block 1 to 4 of the 6 sectors.
* Player Glider: A semi-transparent cockpit visor or glider wings anchored at the 
  bottom of the viewport. A small glow indicator shows the currently occupied lane.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Start Screen: We initially tried to have players align their head with a 
   "CALIBRATE & START" ring, but it needed changing to a more standard menu flow. 
   Now, a "Calibrate & Launch" button (2D HTML and 3D VR box) zero-points the gyro 
   and starts the flight.
2. Speeding Up: The tunnel wireframes start sliding backwards, giving the illusion 
   of forward velocity.
3. Obstacles: Obstacles spawn at Z = -55 (far away) in random lane configurations.
4. Evasion: The player tilts their head left or right to move their glider to one 
   of the six lanes (sectors 0 to 5).
5. Collision Check: When an obstacle reaches the player's depth (Z = -2.5):
   - We initially tried a 1-hit game over upon collision, but it needed changing 
     to a 3-point shield system to increase player leniency and improve the overall gameplay feel. 
     If the player is in a blocked lane, they lose 1 shield cell (flashing the cockpit red) 
     and trigger a game over only when shields reach 0.
   - If the player is in an open lane, play a "pass" whoosh sound, increment the score.
6. Score: Rises continually as they survive. The obstacles speed up over time.

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Cockpit & Camera:
  We initially tried a basic camera rig, but it needed changing to support VR controllers 
  and prevent default desktop dragging conflicts:
  `<a-entity id="rig" position="0 1.6 0">`
    `<a-camera id="camera" look-controls="pointerLockEnabled: false; mouseEnabled: false; touchEnabled: false" wasd-controls="enabled: false">`
      `<!-- VR Cockpit Visor & 3D HUD elements (attached to camera) -->`
    `</a-camera>`
    `<a-entity id="left-hand" laser-controls="hand: left" raycaster="objects: .target; far: 5"></a-entity>`
    `<a-entity id="right-hand" laser-controls="hand: right" raycaster="objects: .target; far: 5"></a-entity>`
  `</a-entity>`

* Hexagonal Lanes:
  Define 6 angles: 0, 60, 120, 180, 240, 300 degrees.
  We initially tried to map only the Y-axis rotation (yaw), but it needed changing 
  to combine both yaw and roll for a more intuitive head tilting feel:
  `const steerDeflection = (yawDeg * yawSensitivity) + (rollDeg * rollSensitivity);`
  If the deflection exceeds a small dead zone, we map it to the nearest 60-degree sector.

* Spawning Obstacles:
  - Create a pool of obstacle entities (`<a-entity class="obstacle">`) shaped like 
    pie wedges or simple thin boxes rotated to block specific lanes.
  - Animate obstacles using a custom tick handler:
    `tick: function (time, timeDelta) {`
      `this.el.object3D.position.z += speed * (timeDelta / 1000);`
      `if (this.el.object3D.position.z >= 0) {`
        `checkCollision(this.el);`
        `resetObstacle(this.el);`
      `}`
    `}`
