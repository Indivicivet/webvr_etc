================================================================================
GAME DESIGN SKETCH: NEON WHACK (0001)
================================================================================

1. HIGH CONCEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A casual arcade Whack-a-Mole clone styled with vibrant, retro-futuristic neon
aesthetics. The player sits or stands in a fixed position, facing a 3x3 vertical 
grid of ports. Glowing energy cores randomly pop out of these ports, and the 
player must deactivate them before they retreat.

2. HARDWARE CONSTRAINTS & ACCESSIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Controls: VR controllers (trigger zapping) or desktop mouse clicks. 
  - Note: We initially tried 100% gaze-only controls with a 300ms fuse reticle, 
    but it needed changing to support active VR controllers and standard mouse click 
    interactions to remove accidental triggers and provide a punchier arcade feel.
* Field of View (FoV): Designed for low-to-medium FoV headsets (<= 90 degrees).
  - Note: We initially tried placing the console board floating 2.4 meters away, 
    but it needed changing (moved to 2.8 meters and scaled down to 85%) to fit 
    comfortably in standard headset screens and desktop views.
  - Side HUD panels are mounted close to the left/right borders of the board 
    to remain easily visible within micro-movements of the player's view.
* Comfort: Stationary, seated experience. The static grid provides a strong spatial 
  anchor, preventing motion sickness.

3. VISUAL LAYOUT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Background: A dark, starless void with a subtle horizontal grid pattern on the 
  floor and ceiling to establish depth.
* Console / Grid: A metallic dark gray panel floating 2.8 meters in front of the 
  player, tilted slightly upward (about 10 degrees) to meet the player's natural 
  resting view.
* Side HUD Panels: Nested directly next to the board borders. Left panel displays 
  Score & Best, right panel displays Integrity/Lives.
* Ports: 9 circular indentations arranged in a 3x3 grid on the panel.
* Cores: Cylindrical capsules that rise from the ports. They glow with an intense, 
  pulsing color:
    - Pink: Standard Core (1 point; costs 1 life if missed)
    - Green: Power Core (3 points, pops up faster; costs 1 life if missed)
    - Orange: Hazard Core (deducts 1 life if clicked/triggered! Safe to let it retreat)
      - Note: We initially tried deducting life just by looking at a hazard core, 
        but it was frustrating and needed changing to require deactivating it to 
        penalize the player.

4. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Start Screen: Player faces a large glowing "START" button. They click or pull 
   their trigger to begin.
   - Note: We initially tried gaze-fusing on start buttons, but it needed changing 
     to direct clicks/triggers to feel more responsive.
2. Active State: A random port spawns a Core. A ticking sound speed increases as 
   time runs out.
3. Player Action: Player aims at the Core with a VR controller (trigger to zap) 
   or desktop mouse (click to zap).
4. Success: If the core is zapped before it retreats:
     - Play a chord chime hit synth sound and a laser shoot sound.
     - Play a colored expanding whack ripple ring around the node.
     - Core retreats. Add points to total score.
5. Failure: If a Standard or Power core retreats undetected:
     - Play a descending miss sweep sound and flash the console red.
     - Play a red expanding miss ring around the node.
     - Lose 1 life.
6. Game Over: Occurs when lives reach 0 (start with 3). Display final score, high 
   score, and a "RESTART" button (click/trigger to reboot).

5. IMPLEMENTATION SPECIFICATIONS (A-FRAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Scene-Level Click Raycaster (Desktop fallback):
  `<a-scene cursor="rayOrigin: mouse" raycaster="objects: .target">`

* VR Tracked Controllers:
  `<a-entity id="left-hand" laser-controls="hand: left" raycaster="objects: .target; far: 5; showLine: true;"></a-entity>`
  `<a-entity id="right-hand" laser-controls="hand: right" raycaster="objects: .target; far: 5; showLine: true;"></a-entity>`

* Ports/Cores Structure:
  Represent the grid with a set of 9 static parent entities (the ports). Inside each
  parent, place a child entity (the core cylinder) and two flat rings for whack/miss 
  animations:
  `<a-ring class="ripple-ring" visible="false" animation__scale="startEvents: whack" ...>`
  `<a-ring class="miss-ring" visible="false" animation__scale="startEvents: miss" ...>`

* Scripting Logic:
  - Keep a single JavaScript file managing state: `score`, `lives`, and `gameActive`.
  - Use `leftHand.addEventListener('controllerconnected', ...)` to switch texts and 
    adapt instruction prompts dynamically when controllers are in use.
  - Play custom AudioContext synth notes on laser shots, zaps, misses, and alerts.
